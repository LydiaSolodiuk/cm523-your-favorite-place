Boston's Freedom Trail

Follow the red bricks through downtown to discover Boston’s revolutionary history. Discover the important people and places that helped found the United States by visiting all the sites along Boston’s Freedom Trail. It is a great way to explore downtown and the North End especially in the summertime. Great for families, The Freedom Trail is flexible to visit with many sites free or low-cost to enter.

Visitor Tips

  • To diversify your historical exploration, consider adding in some sites on the African American history trail in the Beacon Hill area.
  • For a more exciting experience, plan to visit the Freedom Trail on a day when there are historical reenactments or special events like the Fourth of July.
  • While most tourists hop on the T, Boston’s subway system, MBTA buses are often a more efficient way to get around downtown. Try out Bus 4 which loops around the North End.
  • Research restaurants beforehand to avoid overpriced tourist traps. In particular, eat somewhere besides Faneuil Hall.
  • Want more early U.S. history? Hop across the Charles River to Charlestown to see the USS Constitution and the Bunker Hill Monument.
