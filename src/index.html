Boston's Freedom Trail

Follow the red bricks through downtown to discover Boston’s revolutionary history.

Discover the important people and places that helped found the United States by visiting all the sites along Boston’s Freedom Trail. It is a great way to explore downtown and the North End especially in the summertime. Great for families, The Freedom Trail is flexible to visit with many sites free or low-cost to enter.

  1. Old South

    A helpful starting point with a museum that gives a great overview of Boston’s role in the Revolutionary War. Pro tip: one of the only sites with bathrooms.

    Visit Old South

  2. Granary Burying Ground

    An 18th century graveyard with many of Boston’s famous figures including Paul Revere, John Hancock, and the 5 Boston Massacre victims.

    Learn more About Granary Burying Ground

  3. Old North Church

    Step into history at Old North Church with a beautifully restored building designed to look as it did in 1775 during the British occupation of Boston. They also have crypt and steeple tours as well as a reconstructed 18th century print shop.

    Visit Old North Church

  4. Boston Common

    The center of historic Boston, the Common has many monuments scattered around its perimeter. Perfect spot for a picnic!

    Learn More About Boston Common

  5. Paul Revere House

    Enter the home of Paul Revere, 18th century silversmith and one of the Sons of Liberty. He was immortalized in Longfellow’s poem.

    Visit Revere House

Visitor Tips

  • To diversify your historical exploration, consider adding in some sites on the African American history trail in the Beacon Hill area.
  • For a more exciting experience, plan to visit the Freedom Trail on a day when there are historical reenactments or special events like the Fourth of July.
  • While most tourists hop on the T, Boston’s subway system, MBTA buses are often a more efficient way to get around downtown. Try out Bus 4 which loops around the North End.
  • Research restaurants beforehand to avoid overpriced tourist traps. In particular, eat somewhere besides Faneuil Hall.
  • Want more early U.S. history? Hop across the Charles River to Charlestown to see the USS Constitution and the Bunker Hill Monument.

Trivia

What is the real name of Bunker Hill? Breed’s Hill

How many lanterns did Paul Revere see in the steeple of the Old North Church? 2

What year did the Boston Tea Party occur? 1773

What wharf did Abigail Adams use to board her boart to visit her husband overseas during the Revolutionary War? Rowes' Wharf
